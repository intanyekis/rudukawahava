Is Lil Mama and Bow Wow Related? The Long-Running Joke About the Two Rappers

Who is Lil Mama?

Lil Mama is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and actress who rose to fame with her hit single “Lip Gloss” in 2007. She has released two studio albums, VYP (Voice of the Young People) in 2008 and Take Me Back in 2015. She is also known for her role as Lisa “Left Eye” Lopes in the biopic CrazySexyCool: The TLC Story in 2013

Lil Mama was born as Niatia Jessica Kirkland on October 4, 1989, in Harlem, New York. She grew up in Brooklyn, where she developed her passion for music and dance. She started rapping at the age of 10 and recorded her first song, “M.A.N.”, at 13. She adopted the stage name Lil Mama after being inspired by her mother, who died of cancer in 2007. She signed with Jive Records in 2006 and released her debut single, “Lip Gloss”, which peaked at number 10 on the Billboard Hot 100. She then released her debut album, VYP (Voice of the Young People), which featured guest appearances from Chris Brown, T-Pain, and Avril Lavigne. She also became a judge on the reality show America’s Best Dance Crew from 2008 to 2012

Who is Bow Wow?

Bow Wow is an American rapper, actor, and television host who started his career as Lil’ Bow Wow under the mentorship of Snoop Dogg and Jermaine Dupri. He has released seven studio albums, including the platinum-selling Beware of Dog in 2000 and Wanted in 2005. He has also starred in several films, such as Like Mike, Roll Bounce, and The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift. He is currently the host of the BET show 106 & Park

Bow Wow was born as Shad Gregory Moss on March 9, 1987, in Columbus, Ohio. He was discovered by Snoop Dogg at the age of six, who gave him the stage name Lil’ Bow Wow. He then signed with So So Def Recordings and released his debut album, Beware of Dog, at 13. The album spawned the hit singles “Bounce with Me”, “Bow Wow (That’s My Name)”, and “Ghetto Girls”. He then released his second album, Doggy Bag, in 2001, which featured the singles “Thank You” and “Take Ya Home”. He dropped the “Lil’” from his name in 2003 and released his third album, Unleashed, which marked his transition to a more mature sound. He then released his fourth album, Wanted, in 2005, which included the smash hits “Let Me Hold You” (featuring Omarion) and “Like You” (featuring Ciara). He also ventured into acting, starring in films such as Like Mike, Johnson Family Vacation, Roll Bounce, and The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift. He also appeared in the television series Entourage and CSI: Cyber. He has also hosted the BET show 106 & Park since 2012
