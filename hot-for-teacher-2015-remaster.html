Overall Meaning

The lyrics to Van Halen's song "Hot For Teacher" are a tongue-in-cheek portrayal of a student's infatuation with his teacher. The song starts with the singer and his friend speculating on how the teacher will look this year, with the singer declaring that he's "hot for teacher." The first verse then depicts the singer's frustration with his uptight teacher, who can't handle his screaming and needs to see him after school. The chorus is a catchy repetition of how much the singer is "hot for teacher."


The second verse switches the perspective to the female student, asking her how she knows the golden rule and calling her bold. The chorus is repeated again, with the addition of some playful scatting before the song ends with the singer's exclamation that he's "hot for teacher."


While the song's lyrics are undeniably humorous, they also touch on some common teenage experiences of wanting what they can't have and finding themselves unexpectedly attracted to people in positions of power. In the end, it's a fun, lighthearted take on a universal adolescent experience.
