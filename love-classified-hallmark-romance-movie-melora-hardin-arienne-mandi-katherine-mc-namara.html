'Love, Classified': 5 things you need to know about Hallmark romantic comedy

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, June 16, 2024

'Love, Classified', a Hallmark original movie, follows a romance novelist who returns home after a lengthy absence to reconcile with her children, who are on their own romantic journeys. It would be fascinating and delightful to see how a mother will guide her children along the road of love.

If you enjoy watching movies filled with romance, love, and passion, you must check out 'A Royal Runaway Romance', 'Mix Up in the Mediterranean', and 'Don't Go Breaking My Heart'.

When does 'Love, Classified' release, and where can you watch it?

'Love, Classified' premieres on Saturday, April 16, at 8/7c on Hallmark Channel. 

What is 'Love, Classified' all about?

Hallmark's official synopsis for the movie reads, "Romance novelist Emilia (Hardin) is a whirlwind who blows back into the lives of her adult children, Taylor (McNamara) and Zach (Lloyd-Jones), under the pretense of a book signing arranged by her hometown’s local book shop. As Emilia tries to reconnect, Taylor and Zach explore new and past relationships through an app that boasts old-fashioned human connection by way of the classified ad. Zach is given a chance to heal old wounds, while Taylor matches with Franki (Mandi), a woman that changes the way she’s always thought about love."

Who stars in 'Love, Classified'?

Melora Hardin

Melora Hardin is an American actress and singer who is most known for her appearances as Jan Levinson on NBC's 'The Office' and Trudy Monk on USA Network's 'Monk', as well as Tammy Cashman on Amazon Prime Video's 'Transparent', for which she was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award. 'The Bold Type', a Freeform comedy-drama that ran from June 2017 to June 2021, starred Hardin as magazine editor-in-chief Jacqueline Carlyle.

Arienne Mandi, Katherine McNamara, Max Lloyd-Jones, Louriza Tronco, and Baraka Rahmani make up the rest of the cast. 

Who are the showrunners?

'Love, Classified' is directed by Stacey N Harding. The film's producer is Charles Cooper. Neal Dodson serves as executive producer, while Lynn Sternberger is the writer of 'Love, Classified'.
