The Meaning Behind The Song: Still Smokin by Snoop Dogg

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, May 26, 2024

Introduction

As a lifelong fan of Snoop Dogg, I’ve always been captivated by the depth and meaning behind his music. One song that has resonated with me recently is “Still Smokin” from his latest album “BODR.” This track not only showcases Snoop’s lyrical prowess but also pays homage to his roots and the influential Death Row Records era.

The Lyrics

The song begins with Snoop recalling a vivid dream in which he was back on Death Row Records, a label that played a significant role in his career. It sets the tone for the rest of the track, as Snoop reflects on his legacy and the impact of his music. The lyrics are infused with his signature wit and laid-back flow, delivering clever wordplay and memorable lines.

Snoop emphasizes his expertise in the rap game, stating that his “game is cold” and he can still “mold” others. He boasts about his success and wealth, referring to himself as a “hundred sixty million dollar man” and playfully highlighting his bionic nature. These lines showcase his confidence and the undeniable influence he holds in the industry.

Throughout the song, Snoop pays tribute to his late friends and collaborators, including Tupac Shakur, Suge Knight, and Nate Dogg. He acknowledges their impact on his life and career, expressing his desire to keep their legacies alive. This reflection reveals Snoop’s loyalty and his deep-rooted connection to the people who have shaped his journey.

The verse also touches upon Snoop’s desire to create and vibe in the studio. He mentions recording his vocals and enjoying the company of his loved ones, specifically mentioning his “cute bitch who loves smokin’ on vapes.” These lines paint a picture of Snoop’s relaxed and carefree persona, emphasizing his love for music and the laid-back lifestyle he embodies.

The outro of the song consists of the simple yet powerful line, “Yeah, yeah, I don’t love you no more.” It serves as a final statement, asserting Snoop’s independence and refusal to conform to others’ expectations. This line reflects his confidence and authenticity, reminding listeners of his unapologetic approach to life and music.

Additional Information

“Still Smokin” is the opening track on Snoop’s 19th album, “BODR.” The beat used in the song is a familiar one for Snoop fans, as it is the same beat from “The Chronic (Intro)” from Dr. Dre’s iconic debut album “The Chronic.” This connection between Snoop and Dr. Dre adds another layer of nostalgia and significance to the track.

The song was produced by DJ Battlecat and written by Snoop Dogg himself. It was released under the label Death Row Records, a label that played a pivotal role in Snoop’s early career. It’s worth mentioning that the song samples “Impeach The President” by The Honey Drippers, showcasing Snoop’s ability to blend old-school funk with his West Coast rap style.

Conclusion

“Still Smokin” is more than just a rap track; it’s a reflection of Snoop Dogg’s journey, his influences, and his unwavering confidence. This song pays homage to the legacy of Death Row Records while showcasing Snoop’s lyrical prowess and laid-back flow. It’s a testament to his longevity in the industry and his ability to stay true to his unique style. “Still Smokin” is a reminder of why Snoop Dogg remains an icon in the world of hip-hop.
